📊 KPI Definitions
KPIDefinition
GDP/Capita PPPGross Domestic Product per person, adjusted for Purchasing Power Parity. Best for comparing real living standards across countries.
GDP GrowthReal annual GDP growth rate (%). Measures economic momentum. Negative = recession.
CPI InflationConsumer Price Index annual change (%). High inflation erodes purchasing power and compresses FMCG volume.
Unemployment% of labor force without work. Affects disposable income and consumer confidence.
Govt DebtGeneral government gross debt as % of GDP. High debt limits fiscal stimulus capacity.
Current AccountTrade + income balance as % of GDP. Surplus = net exporter; deficit = more imports than exports.
Trade Openness(Exports + Imports) / GDP. High = globally integrated economy. Values over 100% are common for trade hubs.
FDI InflowsForeign Direct Investment as % of GDP. Signals investor confidence and growth potential.
Pvt ConsumptionHousehold final consumption expenditure as % of GDP. High = consumer-driven economy, strong FMCG base.
Urbanization% of population living in urban areas. Drives modern trade channel adoption and FMCG premiumization.
Internet% of individuals using the internet. Proxy for digital commerce readiness and omnichannel penetration.
MobileMobile cellular subscriptions per 100 people. Can exceed 100 (multiple SIMs). Enables m-commerce in FMCG.
Gini CoefficientIncome inequality index. 0 = perfect equality; 100 = maximum inequality. High Gini = polarized consumption.
Median AgeYears. Young markets (<30) = volume growth; aging markets (>45) = premiumization, health, convenience.
Big Mac PriceUSD price of a Big Mac. Informal proxy for local price levels and cost of living.
BM Index vs USBig Mac price vs the US as %. Negative = currency undervalued vs USD; positive = overvalued.

⚠ Important Limitations

🔸 Not for official citation. This dashboard is built for strategic orientation, not academic or regulatory reporting.

🔸 2025 data is forward-looking. Most 2025 values are IMF projections from January 2026 or linear extrapolations.

🔸 Gini is not annually updated. Many countries publish Gini every 3–5 years. Values are carried forward from the latest survey year.

🔸 Big Mac Index has market gaps. Not all 43 markets have consistent semi-annual pricing.

🔸 PPP comparisons shift over time. The IMF updates PPP baselines periodically.

🔸 Unemployment definitions vary. Nigeria's figures reflect methodological changes in 2021. Use EEMEA unemployment data directionally.

🎯 Suggested Use Cases

✅ Client briefings: Use Compare view to show how a client's key market stacks up regionally on GDP growth or inflation.

✅ Market prioritization: Combine GDP growth + urbanization + internet penetration to rank expansion opportunities.

✅ Risk flagging: Govt Debt + CPI + Current Account together signal macro stress. Turkey, Argentina, Egypt are textbook cases.

✅ FMCG channel strategy: High mobile + internet + urbanization = digital/modern trade opportunity.

✅ Competitive context: GDP/Capita PPP + Gini together explain why some markets support premiumization while others are volume-driven.
